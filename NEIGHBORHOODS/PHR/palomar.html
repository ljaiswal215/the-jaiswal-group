ABOUT PALOMAR

ESTATE LIVING. REIMAGINED.

Palomar represents the pinnacle of residential living within Pacific Highlands Ranch. Developed by Toll Brothers beginning in 2019, this gated enclave of sixty-nine luxury homes offers a level of scale, privacy, and architectural refinement that is simply unmatched anywhere else in Carmel Valley.

Access to Palomar is provided through two gated entrances, setting the tone for a community that is as secure as it is striking. Within the gates, homes are positioned along four residential streets — Summit Crest Circle, Lemonglaze Court, Tiki Court, and Kareila Drive — each offering a sense of seclusion and prestige that defines true estate living.

Floor plans in Palomar range from approximately 4,765 to 6,575 square feet, with four to six bedrooms across a mix of single-story and two-story configurations — a rarity in Pacific Highlands Ranch and a significant draw for buyers who prefer single-level living at an estate scale. Grand entrances, soaring ceilings, expansive great rooms, and large sliding glass walls that open to covered loggias bring the best of Southern California’s indoor-outdoor lifestyle into every home. Select homesites exceed half an acre, providing the kind of outdoor space rarely found within a master-planned community.

Canyon views are a hallmark of many Palomar properties, and the natural beauty of the surrounding landscape — which includes more than 1,300 acres of preserved open space across Pacific Highlands Ranch — creates a backdrop that is both dramatic and serene.

Residents of Palomar enjoy exclusive access to The Club at Lopelia Meadows, a private recreation facility featuring a resort-style pool, spa, fitness center, lounge, and children’s wading pool. Solana Ranch Elementary School and Solana Ranch Park are conveniently nearby, as are The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch, miles of trails, and a wide array of shopping and dining options.

The beaches of Del Mar, One Paseo, and Del Mar Highlands Town Center are all within easy reach, and convenient access to Interstate 5 and State Route 56 provides direct connectivity to employment centers throughout San Diego County.

Families in Palomar are served by the Solana Beach School District and San Dieguito Union High School District. Students are generally assigned to Solana Ranch Elementary School, Pacific Trails Middle School, Canyon Crest Academy, and Torrey Pines High School. School assignments should always be confirmed directly with the applicable district.

Palomar is governed by the Palomar at PHR HOA and is subject to applicable Mello-Roos assessments. Contact us for current HOA fee and Mello-Roos information.

For discerning buyers seeking the ultimate expression of Pacific Highlands Ranch living — gated privacy, estate-scale homes, canyon views, and access to an exceptional private club — Palomar stands in a class of its own.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

ABOUT PALOMAR

What schools serve Palomar?

Students residing in Palomar are generally assigned to Solana Ranch Elementary School, Pacific Trails Middle School, and either Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School. The community is served by the Solana Beach School District and San Dieguito Union High School District. As attendance boundaries and enrollment policies may change, we recommend confirming school assignments directly with the applicable school district.

Is Palomar part of an HOA?

Yes. Palomar is a gated community governed by a private HOA. Residents enjoy exclusive access to The Club at Lopelia Meadows, which features a pool, spa, fitness center, lounge, and children’s wading pool. HOA fees, Mello-Roos details, and governing documents vary — contact us for current information.
