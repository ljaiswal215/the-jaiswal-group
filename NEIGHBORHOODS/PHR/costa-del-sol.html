ABOUT COSTA DEL SOL

ESTABLISHED CHARM. EVERYDAY CONVENIENCE.

Costa Del Sol is one of Pacific Highlands Ranch’s most established and accessible single-family home communities. Built by Barratt American in 2002, this neighborhood of 162 detached single-family homes reflects the traditional California architectural influences that were defining the region’s residential character at the turn of the millennium — warm facades, thoughtful streetscapes, and a welcoming sense of community.

The neighborhood’s residential streets include Dogwood Way, Elderberry Way, Guava Way, Oleander Way, Dandelion Way, Foxglove Way, Lavender Way, Chase Way, and Rancho Del Acacia Way — names that evoke the natural landscape of coastal Southern California and contribute to the neighborhood’s distinctive identity within the larger master-planned community.

Floor plans within Costa Del Sol range from approximately 1,615 to 2,191 square feet and typically feature three to four bedrooms. Efficient, well-designed layouts maximize livable space while keeping maintenance manageable — an appealing combination for a broad range of buyers, including those seeking their first single-family home in a premier Carmel Valley location.

Residents of Costa Del Sol enjoy exceptional proximity to the everyday amenities that make Pacific Highlands Ranch such a desirable address. The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch, PHR Community Park, local schools, and an extensive network of hiking and biking trails are all within easy reach. With over 1,300 acres of open space woven throughout the master plan, there is always a trail, a park, or a quiet natural retreat just moments away.

The community’s central location within Pacific Highlands Ranch also provides convenient access to One Paseo, Del Mar Highlands Town Center, and the beaches of Del Mar — allowing residents to enjoy a full coastal California lifestyle without venturing far from home.

Families are drawn to Costa Del Sol in part for its access to excellent public schools. The neighborhood is served by the Del Mar Union School District and San Dieguito Union High School District, with students attending Sycamore Ridge Elementary School, Pacific Trails Middle School, and either Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School.

Costa Del Sol is governed by the Pacific Highlands Ranch Community HOA, which oversees common areas and shared neighborhood amenities throughout the community.

For buyers seeking an established, move-in-ready single-family home in one of Carmel Valley’s most conveniently located and well-regarded master-planned communities, Costa Del Sol represents a compelling opportunity at an accessible price point relative to the broader Pacific Highlands Ranch market.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

ABOUT COSTA DEL SOL

What schools serve Costa Del Sol?

Students residing in Costa Del Sol are generally assigned to Sycamore Ridge Elementary School, Pacific Trails Middle School, and either Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School. The community is served by the Del Mar Union School District and San Dieguito Union High School District. As attendance boundaries and enrollment policies may change, we recommend confirming school assignments directly with the applicable school district.

Is Costa Del Sol part of an HOA?

Yes. Costa Del Sol is governed by the Pacific Highlands Ranch Community HOA, which maintains common areas and shared neighborhood amenities throughout the community. HOA dues and governing documents are subject to change, and we’re happy to provide current information upon request.
