ABOUT SENDERO

MODERN DESIGN. CONNECTED LIVING.

Built by Pardee Homes in 2019, Sendero is a collection of 112 detached single-family homes tucked into the northern portion of Pacific Highlands Ranch. Among the newer neighborhoods in the community, Sendero offers a compelling combination of contemporary design, exceptional walkability, and immediate access to some of PHR’s most valued amenities.

The neighborhood is positioned steps from the West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center and sits directly across from both Solana Ranch Elementary School and Solana Ranch Park — making it one of the most convenient locations in all of Pacific Highlands Ranch for families with school-age children.

Sendero’s residential streets — including Pinewood Trails, Sweetwater Trails, Pacifica Trails, Indigo Sage Drive, Camino Court, Calle Blanca Trails, and Canyon Loop Trail — wind through a walkable neighborhood designed with connection in mind. Residents can easily reach The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch, the park, and the recreation center entirely on foot.

The homes themselves reflect a contemporary aesthetic, featuring a blend of Contemporary and Americana architectural styles characterized by clean lines, open interiors, and welcoming covered patios and private courtyards. Floor plans range from approximately 2,946 to 3,611 square feet and offer four bedrooms alongside flexible bonus spaces that adapt to a wide range of lifestyle needs.

Inside, open-concept great rooms, spacious kitchens, and generous primary suites create an elevated everyday living experience. The combination of thoughtful design and a prime northern PHR location makes Sendero one of the most sought-after neighborhoods in the community.

Sendero residents enjoy full access to the West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center, featuring a Junior Olympic pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, gathering spaces, and a children’s water play area. The surrounding community also offers over 1,300 acres of preserved open space with a network of trails perfect for hiking, biking, and outdoor recreation throughout the year.

Families are drawn to Sendero in part for its access to highly regarded public schools. Students are generally served by the Solana Beach School District and San Dieguito Union High School District, with attendance at Solana Ranch Elementary, Pacific Trails Middle School, and either Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School. School attendance boundaries should always be confirmed directly with the applicable district.

Sendero’s location also provides easy access to the beaches of Del Mar, One Paseo, and Interstate 5 and State Route 56 — making it convenient to reach employment centers and destinations throughout San Diego County.

Sendero is governed by the West Highlands Pacific HOA and is subject to Mello-Roos assessments.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

ABOUT SENDERO

What schools serve Sendero?

Students residing in Sendero are generally assigned to Solana Ranch Elementary School, Pacific Trails Middle School, and either Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School. The community is served by the Solana Beach School District and San Dieguito Union High School District. As attendance boundaries and enrollment policies may change, we recommend confirming school assignments directly with the applicable school district.

Is Sendero part of an HOA?

Yes. Sendero is governed by the West Highlands Pacific HOA. Residents enjoy access to the West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center, which features a Junior Olympic pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, gathering spaces, and a children’s water play area. The community is also subject to Mello-Roos special tax assessments. HOA dues, Mello-Roos details, and governing documents are subject to change, and we’re happy to provide current information upon request.
