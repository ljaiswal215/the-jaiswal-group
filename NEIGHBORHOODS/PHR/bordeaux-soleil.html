ABOUT BORDEAUX & SOLEIL

TWO ESTABLISHED COMMUNITIES. ONE PREMIER LOCATION.

Bordeaux and Soleil at Bordeaux are two of Pacific Highlands Ranch’s original neighborhoods, both developed by Pardee Homes between 2003 and 2006. Together they comprise 406 detached single-family homes in the western portion of PHR — an area that has set the standard for the master-planned community that surrounds them. While closely related in character and location, the two communities have distinct histories, street patterns, school assignments, and homesites worth understanding before you shop.

Bordeaux

As one of the original neighborhoods within Pacific Highlands Ranch, Bordeaux has long been appreciated for its central location, functional floor plans, and exceptional access to many of the community’s most desirable amenities. Developed by Pardee Homes in 2003, this established neighborhood is comprised of 287 single-family residences and continues to be a popular choice for buyers seeking a connected lifestyle in Carmel Valley.

Set less than four miles from the Pacific Ocean, Bordeaux occupies a highly convenient location within Pacific Highlands Ranch. Residents enjoy close proximity to schools, parks, recreation, shopping, dining, and community gathering spaces, creating a lifestyle where many daily destinations are just a short walk, bike ride, or drive away.

The neighborhood is comprised of six residential streets — Gamay Way, Porter Creek Road, Rabbit Ridge Road, Chambord Way, Deer Park Way, and Mill Creek Road. Several homes enjoy elevated canyon-facing locations, offering scenic views and an added sense of privacy. Mature landscaping and established streetscapes contribute to the neighborhood’s welcoming character and enduring appeal.

Homes within Bordeaux offer approximately 2,346 to 2,784 square feet of living space with three to five bedrooms. Thoughtfully designed floor plans provide comfortable spaces for both everyday living and entertaining, while manageable lot sizes create a balance between outdoor enjoyment and low-maintenance living.

One of Bordeaux’s defining advantages is its proximity to many of Pacific Highlands Ranch’s most popular destinations. The neighborhood is located near Sycamore Ridge Elementary School, Cathedral Catholic High School, Pacific Highlands Ranch Community Park, and The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch. Whether attending school events, enjoying community activities, meeting friends for dinner, or running errands, residents benefit from having so many conveniences close to home.

Bordeaux homeowners also enjoy access to the private West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center, one of the community’s most valued amenities. This resort-inspired facility features a Junior Olympic-sized swimming pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, multipurpose gathering spaces, covered outdoor entertaining areas, and a children’s water play area. The recreation center serves as a hub for neighborhood activity and provides opportunities for recreation, relaxation, and connection throughout the year.

Beyond Pacific Highlands Ranch, residents enjoy convenient access to some of North County’s most popular destinations, including One Paseo, Del Mar Highlands Town Center, and the beaches of Del Mar. Nearby Interstate 5 and State Route 56 provide easy connectivity to employment centers throughout San Diego County while maintaining the community’s residential feel.

Families are often drawn to Bordeaux for its access to highly regarded public and private schools. Depending on current attendance boundaries, students may attend schools within the Solana Beach School District and San Dieguito Union High School District. School enrollment and attendance boundaries should always be verified directly with the applicable school district.

For buyers seeking an established neighborhood with a strong sense of community, convenient access to everyday amenities, and the outdoor-oriented lifestyle that defines Pacific Highlands Ranch, Bordeaux continues to be one of Carmel Valley’s most enduring and desirable neighborhoods.

Soleil at Bordeaux

Developed by Pardee Homes in 2006, Soleil at Bordeaux is one of the original neighborhoods that helped shape Pacific Highlands Ranch into one of San Diego’s most sought-after master-planned communities. With 119 detached homes, spacious floor plans, and a location that places schools, recreation, shopping, and dining within easy reach, Soleil at Bordeaux continues to attract buyers seeking the ideal balance of comfort, convenience, and community.

Situated along Briarcrest Place, White Oak Lane, Sonoma Place, Flintwood Way, Sunstone Point, and Chambord Way, the neighborhood enjoys a highly desirable location in the western portion of Pacific Highlands Ranch. Residents are positioned directly across from Cathedral Catholic High School and adjacent to Sycamore Ridge School, creating one of the most convenient school locations within the community.

Homes in Soleil at Bordeaux range from approximately 2,346 to 2,809 square feet and typically offer three to five bedrooms. Designed during a period when Pardee Homes was known for creating highly functional family floor plans, many residences feature spacious kitchens, open-concept living areas, generous primary suites, and flexible spaces that continue to meet the needs of today’s buyers. Homesites can be larger than many of the newer neighborhoods within Pacific Highlands Ranch, with some lots approaching 0.20 acres.

Residents enjoy access to the private West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center, one of the community’s most popular amenities. The recreation center features a Junior Olympic-sized swimming pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, gathering spaces, and a children’s water play area. Whether spending summer afternoons at the pool or gathering with neighbors throughout the year, the recreation center serves as an important extension of community life.

One of Soleil at Bordeaux’s strongest advantages is its location. The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch, community parks, trails, shopping, dining, fitness studios, and everyday conveniences are all located nearby. The neighborhood’s central setting makes it easy to enjoy many of the amenities that have made Pacific Highlands Ranch such a desirable place to live.

Families are particularly drawn to Soleil at Bordeaux for its access to highly regarded schools. Students are typically assigned to Sycamore Ridge School, Pacific Trails Middle School, and Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School, depending on current attendance boundaries and enrollment policies. School enrollment and attendance boundaries should always be verified directly with the applicable school district.

Beyond the neighborhood, residents enjoy convenient access to more than 1,300 acres of preserved open space, miles of interconnected trails, Del Mar beaches, One Paseo, Del Mar Highlands Town Center, Interstate 5, and State Route 56. The community’s location provides an ideal combination of outdoor recreation, coastal access, and everyday convenience.

What distinguishes Soleil at Bordeaux is its combination of established neighborhood character, larger family-oriented homes, and exceptional connectivity to schools and community amenities. As one of Pacific Highlands Ranch’s original neighborhoods, it continues to offer the timeless appeal, livability, and sense of community that have defined the area from the beginning.

Both communities are centrally positioned near The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch, Pacific Highlands Ranch Community Park, One Paseo, and Del Mar Highlands Town Center. Convenient freeway access via Interstate 5 and State Route 56 connects residents to employment centers throughout San Diego County, with Del Mar beaches just a few miles away.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

ABOUT BORDEAUX & SOLEIL

What is the difference between Bordeaux and Soleil at Bordeaux?

Both communities were built by Pardee Homes and share the same schools, HOA, and recreation center. The key differences are home count and year built: Bordeaux has 287 homes (built 2003) and Soleil has 119 homes (built 2006). Soleil homesites tend to be larger, approaching 0.20 acres. Both communities are served by Sycamore Ridge Elementary under the Del Mar Union School District, and both are subject to Mello-Roos special tax assessments. Cathedral Catholic High School (private) is located directly across from Soleil.

Are Bordeaux and Soleil at Bordeaux part of an HOA?

Yes. Both communities are governed by the West Highlands Pacific HOA. Residents enjoy access to the West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center, which features a Junior Olympic pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, covered gathering spaces, and a children’s water play area. Both Bordeaux and Soleil at Bordeaux are subject to Mello-Roos special tax assessments. HOA dues and governing documents are subject to change, and we’re happy to provide current information upon request.

How far are Bordeaux and Soleil from the beach and freeway?

Both communities sit in the western portion of Pacific Highlands Ranch, approximately three to four miles from the Del Mar beaches. Interstate 5 and State Route 56 are both easily accessible, connecting residents to downtown San Diego, UTC, Sorrento Valley, and employment centers throughout the county. One Paseo and Del Mar Highlands Town Center are just minutes away for daily shopping and dining.
