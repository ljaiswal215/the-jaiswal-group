ABOUT BRIGHTWATER

TIMELESS DESIGN. RESORT-INSPIRED LIVING.

Nestled in the northern reaches of Pacific Highlands Ranch, Brightwater is one of Carmel Valley’s most architecturally distinctive single-family home communities. Developed by Pardee Homes in 2011, this intimate neighborhood of 75 homes offers a thoughtful blend of timeless design, generous living spaces, and resort-caliber amenities in one of San Diego’s most sought-after zip codes.

Brightwater stands apart for its architectural diversity, showcasing three distinct home styles — Napa, Spanish, and Coastal Cottage — that give the streetscape a rich, curated character uncommon in newer master-planned communities. Each style reflects careful attention to detail, from the warm earth tones and clay-tile rooflines of the Spanish-inspired homes to the relaxed, light-filled character of the Coastal Cottage designs.

Homes range from approximately 2,827 to 3,432 square feet and typically offer four to five bedrooms, making Brightwater well-suited for growing families who need generous, flexible space. Larger homesites provide additional outdoor living and privacy, a quality that continues to be highly valued throughout the Pacific Highlands Ranch area.

Residents of Brightwater enjoy the peaceful, walkable streets of the community — including Quail Run Street, Montez Villa Road, and Silverbush Creek Street — which wind through a neighborhood designed with connection and comfort in mind.

As part of the West Highlands Pacific HOA, Brightwater residents have access to the West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center, a resort-style amenity hub featuring a Junior Olympic swimming pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, covered gathering spaces, and a dedicated children’s water play area. Miles of extensive trails winding through the surrounding open space provide additional opportunities for outdoor recreation and leisurely walks throughout the year.

Brightwater is served by two well-regarded school districts. Elementary-age students attend Solana Ranch Elementary School within the Solana Beach School District, while middle and high school students are served by the San Dieguito Union High School District, with typical pathways to Pacific Trails Middle School and either Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School. Families are encouraged to confirm current enrollment boundaries directly with the applicable school districts.

The neighborhood’s location in northern Pacific Highlands Ranch provides excellent connectivity. The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch, One Paseo, and the beaches of Del Mar are all within easy reach, as are State Route 56 and Interstate 5, making Brightwater a convenient base for commuting to employment centers throughout San Diego County.

For buyers seeking spacious, architecturally interesting single-family homes in one of Carmel Valley’s most desirable master-planned communities, Brightwater offers an exceptional combination of design, lifestyle, and location.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

ABOUT BRIGHTWATER

What schools serve Brightwater?

Students residing in Brightwater are generally assigned to Solana Ranch Elementary School, Pacific Trails Middle School, and either Canyon Crest Academy or Torrey Pines High School. The community is served by the Solana Beach School District and San Dieguito Union High School District. As attendance boundaries and enrollment policies may change, we recommend confirming school assignments directly with the applicable school district.

Is Brightwater part of an HOA?

Yes. Brightwater is governed by the West Highlands Pacific HOA. Residents enjoy access to the West Highlands Pacific Recreation Center, which features a Junior Olympic pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, covered gathering areas, and children’s water play area. The community is also subject to Mello-Roos special tax assessments. HOA dues, Mello-Roos details, and governing documents are subject to change, and we’re happy to provide current information upon request.
