ABOUT POWAY

WHERE SUBURBAN LIVING FINDS ITS FINEST FORM

Poway proudly carries its longtime nickname — "The City in the Country" — because it describes something genuine: a suburban community of nearly 50,000 residents that has deliberately preserved a rural, spacious character amid the relentless development pressure of greater San Diego. Larger lots, mature landscaping, equestrian-friendly neighborhoods, and an absence of the density that defines most of the county give Poway a lifestyle that attracts buyers who want more room, more quiet, and more connection to the natural environment.

The Poway Unified School District is one of Poway's most celebrated assets — consistently recognized among California's best-performing school districts, it draws families from across the county who prioritize education. Poway High School, Rancho Bernardo High School, and the district's elementary campuses serve the community with programs, facilities, and outcomes that speak for themselves.

Poway Lake, the Blue Sky Ecological Reserve, and Lake Poway Park provide exceptional outdoor recreation just minutes from most residents' front doors — hiking, fishing, picnicking, and trail running in a natural setting that feels worlds away from the urban core.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

COMMON QUESTIONS ABOUT POWAY

What types of homes are available in Poway?

Poway is known for its spacious single-family homes on large lots, a rarity in San Diego County at the price points available here. The city’s housing stock is diverse — ranging from 1960s ranch-style homes in established neighborhoods to custom hillside estates, newer construction in planned communities, and rural residential properties with acreage.

One of Poway’s most appealing characteristics is the prevalence of large lots, mature landscaping, and the sense of space that is increasingly difficult to find closer to the coast. Home prices range from the high-$700s for smaller properties to well over $2 million for larger estates and premium view homes.

Why is Poway called “The City in the Country”?

Poway earned this tagline because of its deliberate commitment to preserving open space and maintaining a rural feel despite being a fully incorporated city within a major metropolitan area. The city has protected significant natural land through open space preserves, and its broader planning philosophy has consistently prioritized green space, equestrian access, and low-density development.

The result is a community that feels genuinely different from the rest of suburban San Diego — quieter, more spacious, more connected to nature — while still offering full urban services and easy access to the broader region via I-15 and SR-56.

What are the schools like in Poway?

Poway is served by the Poway Unified School District, which is consistently ranked among the top school districts in California. The district operates highly regarded elementary, middle, and high schools with strong academic programs, extracurricular opportunities, and college preparation outcomes that attract families specifically seeking top-tier public education.

As always, attendance boundaries can change and specific school assignments should be confirmed directly with Poway Unified before making any home purchase decision based on school access.

Does Poway allow horses?

Yes — Poway is one of the few communities in San Diego County with meaningful equestrian zoning and infrastructure. The city has established equestrian-friendly zones where horses can be kept on residential properties, and there are community riding trails that connect various parts of the city.

If you’re looking for a property where horses can be kept, we can identify listings that are specifically zoned and equipped for equestrian use.
